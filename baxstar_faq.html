QUESTIONS, ANSWERED. — Common Questions About Baxstar Fishing Private Guided Trips, Detroit Lakes, Minnesota: Booking, Equipment, Weather, Family Trips, Corporate Groups, and Minnesota Fishing License.

Real answers to the questions clients ask most before booking a private guided trip on Detroit Lakes — from a 5× Master Angler with 30+ years on these waters.

  1. 01

    What makes Baxstar Fishing different from other Detroit Lakes guides?

    Baxstar is the only fully private guided fishing service in the Detroit Lakes area — every trip is just you and your group, with no shared boats and no rotating clients. Guide Brady Baxter has 30+ years on these waters, 7 years guiding professionally, 5 Master Angler awards, and a 4.9★ rating from 100+ verified clients.

  2. 02

    How far ahead should I book a private guided trip?

    Roughly half of Baxstar trips book within 10 days, but prime summer Saturdays from mid-June through August often book 30 to 60 days out. For a specific date, a weekend, or a multi-day group, two to three months ahead is the safest window. Last-minute openings happen — call Brady directly at (218) 325-6857.

  3. 03

    What is included in a Baxstar guided fishing trip?

    The Vexus DVX20S boat, all fuel, premium rods and reels, fresh live bait, full tackle, life jackets, Garmin LiveScope sonar, hands-on instruction, and catch cleaning when requested. Guests bring a cooler and a valid Minnesota fishing license. Snacks and drinks for the group are welcome — onboard storage handles them.

  4. 04

    Do you guide first-time anglers, kids, or non-fishing partners?

    Yes — many Baxstar trips include first-time anglers, children, or non-fishing partners along for the ride. Brady tailors the pace, the technique, and the species target so beginners catch fish quickly and confidently. The Vexus is comfortable for all ages, and the Detroit Lakes-area waters are calm enough for mixed-experience groups.

  5. 05

    Which lakes do you fish, and how do you choose where to go each day?

    Baxstar covers 100+ lakes across the Detroit Lakes region — including Detroit Lake, Pelican Lake, Lake Sallie, Lake Lida, Big Cormorant Lake, and Upper Cormorant. Brady chooses the lake the morning of your trip based on weather, wind direction, water temperature, and current scouting — optimizing for the bite, not convenience.

  6. 06

    Is the boat truly private, or do I share it with other clients?

    Every Baxstar trip is 100% private. Your group has the boat, the guide, and Brady's full attention for the entire duration of the trip. There are no mixed parties, no shared time blocks, and no rotating between clients. Fully private guided trips are uncommon in the Detroit Lakes area.

  7. 07

    What boat and equipment does Baxstar Fishing use?

    Brady runs a Vexus DVX20S — a tournament-grade fiberglass walleye boat with Garmin LiveScope live-imaging sonar, dual livewells, twin pedestal seats, and a full premium rod-and-reel setup. The Vexus handles big water and small, runs quietly enough for pressured walleyes, and seats four anglers comfortably with room to fish.

  8. 08

    What happens if the weather changes the day of my trip?

    Safety comes first — if conditions are unsafe, the trip is rescheduled at no cost. For minor weather shifts, Brady changes the lake and adjusts the game plan instead of canceling. Trip Protection at checkout permits guest-initiated cancellations up to 24 hours before the trip. Straightforward, transparent, free of fine print.

  9. 09

    Can you arrange a multi-day trip or a corporate group?

    Yes. Multi-boat corporate retreats, multi-day father-son and family weekends, and small executive groups are some of Baxstar's most-requested experiences. Brady coordinates the lake schedule, pairs additional guides when needed, and recommends on-water lodging on Detroit Lake or Pelican Lake. Email info@baxstarfishing.com to plan a private group.

  10. 10

    Do I need a Minnesota fishing license, and where do I get one?

    Every angler aged 16 and over needs a valid Minnesota fishing license. Pick one up online in under five minutes at the Minnesota DNR website, at any Detroit Lakes bait shop, or at local outfitters. A 24-hour, 72-hour, or season license all qualify — bring a digital or printed copy to the boat.
